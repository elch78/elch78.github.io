- Title: boring is exciting
- back in the days when I was leading raids in World of Warcraft someone told me that my voice has something soothing in it
- as a tutor that might not be the best attribute to present a topic
- in software engineering being boring is a good thing though
- good software is boring. It fulfils its job. It doesn't surprise you. It is easy to read. It is easy to understand. And thus it is easy to work with. And finally since we don't live in an idealistic world: software that is easy to work with is cheap.
- a good example of a good piece of software from my career was the Corona Warn app. It was an app that was designed by some big players in Germany at the time with a very clear
goal: To allow mass monitoring of Covid cases and tracking the contacts of a person without creating a big brother tool.
- I remember when I started to dig into the project that the documentation of the project was so good that I was able to read
it and understand the whole concept in may one or two hours. My takeaway is still that the concept made 100% sense and that I had the
feeling that I understood most of it. It was a moment of excitement and joy - but it was boring at the same time.
- It also left me with the impression that the seemingly impossible task of tracking peoples contacts without infringing on their privacy was possible. I thought that the app was an invaluable tool in
the fight against contagious diseases that could pose a great benefit to public health. History chose a different path sadly. 
- I ended up not digging into the code too much, but that moment stuck with me as a perfect example of how good software and documentation should look like. Kudos to the
creators of that piece of software (I think it was Telekom).
- I'll leave it with that "shoverthought" and maybe foollow it up with some examples of some practices that help me in creating boring software.
