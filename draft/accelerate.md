Astonishingly, these results demonstrate that there is no tradeoff between improving performance and achieving higher
levels of stability and quality. Rather, high performers do better at all of these measures. This is precisely what the
Agile and Lean movements predict, but much dogma in our industry still rests on the false assumption that moving faster
means trading off against other performance goals, rather than enabling and reinforcing them.4

In software organizations, the ability to work and deliver in small batches is especially important, because it allows
you to gather user feedback quickly using techniques such as A/B testing. It’s worth noting that the ability to take an
experimental approach to product development is highly correlated with the technical practices that contribute to
continuous delivery.
